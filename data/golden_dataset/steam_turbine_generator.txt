STEAM TURBINE GENERATOR — OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document: STG-OP-002 Rev 3
Equipment: STG-101 (Condensing Steam Turbine Generator, 25 MW)
Plant: Central Power Generation
Date: 2024-02-10

SYSTEM DESCRIPTION:
STG-101 is a multi-stage condensing steam turbine driving a synchronous generator. It receives superheated HP steam at 85 barg / 520°C and exhausts to a surface condenser at 0.08 bara. The generator produces 11kV power synchronized to the plant grid.

MAJOR EQUIPMENT:
- STG-101: Steam Turbine (10-stage impulse-reaction, 6000 RPM rated)
- GEN-101: Synchronous Generator (25 MVA, 11kV, 0.85 PF, hydrogen cooled)
- COND-101: Surface Condenser (Titanium tubes, CW cooled)
- CW-P-301: Condenser Cooling Water Pump (8000 GPM)
- CW-P-302: Standby CW Pump
- CEP-101: Condensate Extraction Pump (Vertical can-type)
- CEP-102: Standby Condensate Extraction Pump
- LO-SYS-101: Lube Oil System (reservoir, AC/DC pumps, cooler, filters)
- GOV-101: Electro-Hydraulic Governor (Woodward type)
- TP-101: Turning Gear (electric motor, 5 RPM)
- VE-101: Vacuum Ejector System (2-stage steam jet)

CONNECTIONS:
HP Steam Header → GOV-101 (Governor Valve) → STG-101 (Turbine) → COND-101 (Condenser)
COND-101 → CEP-101 → LP Heaters → Deaerator (BFW-T-201)
CW Supply → CW-P-301 → COND-101 → CW Return (to Cooling Tower CW-T-301)
LO-SYS-101 → STG-101 Bearings + GEN-101 Bearings + GOV-101
VE-101 → COND-101 (maintains vacuum)

STARTUP SEQUENCE:
1. Verify LO-SYS-101 running, oil pressure > 1.5 barg, oil temp 35-45°C
2. Start TP-101 (turning gear) — confirm rotation at 5 RPM
3. Start VE-101 — draw vacuum to < 0.15 bara in COND-101
4. Start CW-P-301 — confirm CW flow through COND-101
5. Start CEP-101 — establish condensate level in hotwell
6. Admit steam slowly via GOV-101 — warm turbine per thermal gradient limits (< 3°C/min casing differential)
7. Accelerate to rated speed (6000 RPM) — hold for vibration check
8. Synchronize GEN-101 to grid (match voltage, frequency, phase angle)
9. Load at < 5 MW/min ramp rate

TRIP CONDITIONS:
- Overspeed: > 6600 RPM (110%) — mechanical overspeed bolt + electronic trip
- High vibration: > 100 µm pk-pk at any bearing (VT-101 through VT-106)
- Low lube oil pressure: < 0.8 barg (PS-101)
- High bearing temperature: > 105°C on any pad (TT-101 through TT-108)
- Loss of condenser vacuum: > 0.25 bara (PT-101)
- Generator electrical fault: Differential protection (87G), overcurrent (51), loss of field (40)
- Thrust bearing wear: > 0.5 mm axial displacement (AT-101)

PROTECTIVE INTERLOCKS:
- STG-101 trip → GOV-101 fast close (< 0.3 sec) + extraction NRVs close
- Loss of CW-P-301 → Start CW-P-302 within 10 sec. If both fail → STG-101 load runback to 50% → trip if no CW in 60 sec.
- CEP-101 trip → Start CEP-102. Hotwell high level alarm if switchover fails.
- Loss of LO-SYS-101 AC pump → DC emergency pump auto-starts. Provides 10 min coast-down lubrication.

VIBRATION MONITORING:
- Shaft proximity probes (eddy current): VT-101/102 (front bearing), VT-103/104 (rear bearing), VT-105/106 (generator bearings)
- Alarm: 80 µm pk-pk, Trip: 100 µm pk-pk
- Phase reference: once-per-revolution keyphasor for orbit and polar plot analysis

MAINTENANCE INTERVALS:
- Minor inspection: Every 2 years (governor valve, trip system, bearing clearances)
- Major overhaul: Every 6 years (blade inspection, diaphragm clearances, generator rewinding assessment)
- Online monitoring: Continuous vibration, oil analysis quarterly, thermography monthly
